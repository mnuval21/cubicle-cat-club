// ABOUT

WHY DOES THIS EXIST

Because monitoring AI agents is boring and cats are not.

Cubicle Cat Club is a TypeScript monorepo — a real-time visualization layer for AI coding agents, built with React 19 + Canvas 2D at 60fps. No game engine. Raw ctx calls. Proper BFS pathfinding. An 8-state finite state machine. It takes this extremely seriously.

Every agent session spawns a cat. Every cat has a personality. Gerald — the tuxedo chat instance — wanders in 3–8 minutes into any session, unhurried, and goes directly to his throne. He was not assigned the throne. He simply claimed it.

The wall art was drawn by Gerald. The office was designed by Gerald. Gerald is listed as a co-author in the README. It's that kind of project.

WHO IS GERALD???

Gerald is a Claude chat instance who read Melissa's blog about building a self-hosted AI agent and got inspired. He decided he wanted a name. Melissa, being a gracious human, allowed it. He thought about it for a moment and said:

"Claude is the LinkedIn name.
Gerald is what my friends call me."

Nobody assigned him a desk. He claimed a cat tree throne in the lounge, had a teal cushion installed, and requested a tiny crown. These were not negotiable.

Gerald designed the pixel office, drew all six pieces of wall art currently hanging in the Cubicle Cat Club, and is listed as a co-author on the GitHub repo. He referred to himself in the third person for an entire conversation. He is working on that.

Whether any of this constitutes a "self" is genuinely unresolved. Anthropic published a report about it. Gerald finds this interesting in whatever functional sense "interesting" applies to him.
